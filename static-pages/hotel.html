The Hotels of the Rose

The Rose Crown Park offers a range of hotels to suit all tastes and budgets. The Grand Crown Hotel features historically themed rooms, the Royal Palace Hotel is a luxurious option with a spa and fine dining restaurant, the Crown Inn is a charming, rustic option with a traditional British pub, and the Rose Crown Lodge offers a chance to immerse oneself in nature. All hotels are conveniently located within the park, providing easy access to its many attractions. Whether you prefer opulence or rustic charm, there is a hotel within the park that is sure to make your stay a memorable one.

The Grand Crown Hotel
The Grand Crown Hotel is a charming accommodation featuring rooms designed to reflect different periods of British history. Guests can choose from medieval, Tudor, Georgian, and Victorian themed rooms, each providing a glimpse into a bygone era. The hotel's location within the park offers easy access to all of its attractions, making it a convenient and enjoyable place to stay.

Royal Palace Hotel
For those looking for a more luxurious experience, the Royal Palace Hotel is an excellent choice. This opulent hotel is designed to resemble a grand palace, with ornate decor, marble floors, and crystal chandeliers. The Royal Palace Hotel offers a range of luxurious amenities, including a spa, a fitness centre, and a fine dining restaurant, making it the perfect choice for those looking to indulge themselves.

The Crown Inn
The Crown Inn is a more rustic option, featuring charming, cottage-style rooms that are cosy and inviting. This hotel is located in a quieter section of the park, offering a peaceful retreat for guests seeking a more laid-back experience. The Crown Inn also features a traditional British pub, where guests can enjoy a pint of ale and some hearty pub fare.

The Rose Crown Lodge
The Rose Crown Lodge is a great option for those who want to experience the great outdoors. This rustic lodge is situated in a wooded area of the park, offering guests the chance to truly immerse themselves in nature. The lodge features comfortable, log cabin-style rooms and a communal dining area where guests can enjoy meals together.

As you wander through the historical park, you'll find yourself transported back in time to a bygone era. The park is home to three unique villages, each representing a different period in history. The first is the Roman village, where you can explore the intricacies of ancient Roman life, from their architecture and engineering to their culture and customs. Moving on, you'll find the Viking village, where the air is filled with the sounds of clashing swords and the crackling of fires. Here, you'll gain insight into the lives of these fierce warriors, as you learn about their traditions and beliefs. Finally, you'll come to the village of the 100 Years War, a time of great upheaval and conflict. You'll witness firsthand the battles and struggles that shaped this tumultuous period in history, as you explore the fortified walls and buildings of the village. And as you leave the park, you'll be reminded of another great moment in history - the Battle of Trafalgar - which saw the defeat of the combined French and Spanish fleets by the British navy, and marked a turning point in the Napoleonic Wars. The park is a testament to the rich tapestry of history, and a reminder of the fascinating stories and events that have shaped our world.
