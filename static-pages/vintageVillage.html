The Villages of the Rose

Welcome to our historical park where you can experience life in different eras of history. Explore our Roman village, where you can wander through streets lined with ancient buildings and witness daily life in a bustling Roman town. Take a stroll through our Viking village, where you can see skilled craftsmen at work and witness battles between fierce warriors. Step back in time to the Hundred Years War and experience life in a medieval village, where you can see knights in shining armor and watch as archers defend their castle walls. Finally, visit our village from the XIXe centuries and witness the daily life of sailors and the intense naval battles that shaped the course of history. With so much to discover and explore, our historical park is a must-visit for anyone interested in history and the fascinating cultures and events that have shaped our world.

The Roman Village
In the Roman village, you'll see the impressive architecture and engineering feats that defined this civilization. Marvel at the grandeur of the aqueduct, imagine yourself as a gladiator in the arena, and explore the marketplace where merchants sell exotic goods from all corners of the empire.

The XIX centuries Village

As you wander through the historical park, you'll find yourself transported back in time to a bygone era. The park is home to three unique villages, each representing a different period in history. The first is the Roman village, where you can explore the intricacies of ancient Roman life, from their architecture and engineering to their culture and customs. Moving on, you'll find the Viking village, where the air is filled with the sounds of clashing swords and the crackling of fires. Here, you'll gain insight into the lives of these fierce warriors, as you learn about their traditions and beliefs. Finally, you'll come to the village of the 100 Years War, a time of great upheaval and conflict. You'll witness firsthand the battles and struggles that shaped this tumultuous period in history, as you explore the fortified walls and buildings of the village. And as you leave the park, you'll be reminded of another great moment in history - the Battle of Trafalgar - which saw the defeat of the combined French and Spanish fleets by the British navy, and marked a turning point in the Napoleonic Wars. The park is a testament to the rich tapestry of history, and a reminder of the fascinating stories and events that have shaped our world.
